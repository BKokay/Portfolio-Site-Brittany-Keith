About

My Story

Originally from Atlanta, GA, USA, I moved to Bonn, Germany in the cold, dark winter of 2020. Two months later, Germany went into lockdown. With no way to get out and meet people, I decided to use the imposed free time to explore a career change from teaching into tech.

Starting with a course in UX Design, I found joy in learning about usability and usefullness in products and services. Training my eye to digest the UX of a website took a lot of practice, but after 15 months of study, I finally feel confident in my judgement.

At the end of my UX Design course, I took a short specialization course titled Frontend Development for Designers (where I built this portfolio site!) I quickly fell in love with the gratification of seeing something being built and instantly work. Okay, maybe not instantly work, but it always does eventually, right? I'm continuing to learn programming with the goal of working in a role that is a combination of UX and Development.

Outside of the hours I dedicate to coding, I take German classes, explore the hiking and biking trails around the Rhein with my family, and try different recipes from my growing collection of cookbooks.
