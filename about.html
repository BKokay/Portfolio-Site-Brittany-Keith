About

My Story

I’m Brittany, but I prefer to go by BK so that I don’t get confused with the other Britney.

I’m originally from Atlanta, GA, USA and moved to Bonn, Germany in the cold, dark winter of 2020 right before everything shut down due to The Pandemic . Seizing the mandatory hours spent at home as an opportunity, I decided to dive deep into my lifelong dream of having a career in something “techie”. In my search, I discovered UX design and FELL IN LOVE.

I love when things just “work” and when I have a seemless experience. My young adult years were spent bartending in an environment that taught me to pay attention to details, right down serving a bottle of beer with the label artfully turned torwards the customer. I learned the joy that comes from delighting people with good experiences.

I later worked as a teacher which taught me another valuable skill for UX - iteration. There is no such thing as a perfect lesson due to students all being at different levels, so iteration (or scaffolding & differentiation in teacher talk) must be part of a good teacher’s wheel house. I learned that you can’t get stuck on one idea of how things should work; you have to listen to the feedback your given and adjust based on what users need.

In learning the design thinking process, I’ve realized that constant learning is what lights me up inside . In addition to the constant task of reskilling as a designer, I am also on a journey to learn the language of code. I’ve spent thousands of hours learning Spanish, German, and Korean, so I am excited to learn a language that can build something besides conversations.
